Mount Rushmore



Mount Rushmore National Memorial is a sculpture carved into the granite face of Mount Rushmore. Mount Rushmore features 60-foot sculptures of the heads of four United States presidents: George Washington, Thomas Jefferson, Theodore Roosevelt, and Abraham Lincoln. The construction started in October 4, 1927 and opened October 31, 1941. Its opened from about 5am to 12pm. It is a great place to visit and bring your family to. The site also features a museum with interactive exhibits.

The White House



The White House is a historic home and office of the United States president, with tours for visitors. Construction started October 13,1792 and opened around the 1800's. The height is 70. Almost every president has lived in the White House.

Niagara Falls



Niagara Falls is the name for three waterfalls that straddle the international border between Canada and the United States. It has beautiful views and a spectacular place to go. From largest to smallest, the three waterfalls are the Horseshoe Falls, the American Falls and the Bridal Veil Falls. It has a vertical drop of more than 165 feet. It is such a beautiful place to visit.

The Grand Canyon



The Grand Canyon is a steep-sided canyon carved by the Colorado River in the state of Arizona in the United States.The Grand Canyon is 277 miles long, up to 18 miles wide and attains a depth of over a mile. It has been around for about 5-6 million years. Since that time, the Colorado River has driven the down-cutting of the tributaries and retreat of the cliffs, simultaneously deepening and widening the canyon. It is again, a wonderful place to visit.

The Statue of Liberty



The Statue of Liberty is a sculpture on Liberty Island in New York Harbor in New York City, in the United States. The height of the statue is 151 feet 1 inch. It was designed by Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi, a French sculptor, was built by Gustave Eiffel and it was unveiled in October 28, 1886. There has ben 3.2 million visitors in 2009.
